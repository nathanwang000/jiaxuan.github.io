Jiaxuan Wang (王家轩)

Me at Grand Canyon, 2014
I'm a rising second year PhD student at the Unversity of Michigan, adviced by professor Jenna Wiens.

My research interest lies in model interpretabilty. I'd like to build machine learning models that are easy to debug and can incorporate expert background knowledge that are beyond data (e.g., causal knowledge).

Born in Nancheng and raised in Beijing, went to University of Michigan as undergrad (2013-2017), I'm a huge fan of basketball (both play and watch) and music performance (practiced violin since 5, picked up guitar at 21). I also do rock climbing, swimming, and juggling. I love watching movies and would like to write a movie script in the future.

Previous worked as an undergrad researcher on extending Convnet with rotation equivariance under the supervision of professor Jia Deng (2014-2015). I aslo worked as a machine learning intern at Bloomberg L.P. (2016) on parsing and ranking natural language query for financial charts under supervision of Dr. Konstantine Arkoudas (became a huge fan on functional programming) and Dr. Srivas Prasad.
